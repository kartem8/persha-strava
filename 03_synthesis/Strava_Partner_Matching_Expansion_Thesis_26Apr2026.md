# Strava Partner Matching Expansion Thesis

## Purpose

Цей документ фіксує оновлену expansion-thesis, яка виросла з ідеї `Strava Courts / Social Sports`, але тепер формулюється ширше:

- не тільки `court sports`
- а `partner matching / training coordination layer`
- для social sports, selected outdoor activities і частини gym use cases

## Core Reframe

Початкова ідея:

- `Strava Courts / Social Sports`

Оновлена, сильніша формулювання:

- `Strava Partner Layer`

### Product statement

Strava допомагає людям без зайвого тертя знаходити собі пару або невелику групу для тренувань за:

- sport type
- level
- pace / intensity
- time availability
- location
- session format

## Why This Direction Matters

Головна теза:

- люди часто не тренуються не лише через брак мотивації
- а через те, що `нема з ким`

Це робить partner matching не “приємним соціальним бонусом”, а реальним friction-removal layer.

## Supporting Signals

## 1. Strava’s own data supports social exercise as a core motivator

Strava у своєму `Year In Sport` messaging прямо підкреслює:

- across generations, the number one reason people exercise with others is `social connection`
- over half of Strava athletes say they are most motivated by friends or family members who exercise
- `77%` of Gen Z athletes say they feel more connected to others when seeing friends’ or family’s activities on Strava
- Gen Z is also more likely to exercise with others

### Main implication

Strava already has evidence that training with others is not edge behavior — it is central behavior.

Source:

- [Strava releases Year In Sport Trend Report](https://press.strava.com/articles/strava-releases-year-in-sport-trend-report)

## 2. A missing partner is a real barrier to exercise

The Gym Group highlights that a large share of people would feel more motivated if they had a gym partner.

The broader implication is simple:

- `no partner` is often a real barrier to starting or maintaining exercise

This matters especially for:

- gym beginners
- people returning after a pause
- social sport players
- users who want accountability without joining a full club

Source:

- [The benefits of working out with your gym partner](https://www.thegymgroup.com/blog/the-benefits-of-working-out-with-your-gym-partner/)

## 3. Exercise is increasingly social, not just functional

Les Mills’ wellness framing reinforces that fitness is becoming a more central part of people’s lifestyle identity.

That does not prove partner matching on its own, but it strengthens the logic that:

- products that reduce friction into real-world movement can become highly habitual

Source:

- [Wellness wins: why 94% of members can't live without their gym](https://www.lesmills.com/articles/wellness-wins-why-94-of-members-cant-live-without-their-gym)

## 4. Safety is a real layer, especially for women and outdoor contexts

Trail-running safety research shows that women report stronger concern around:

- lack of cell service
- getting lost
- harassment or unwanted interactions

### Main implication

For some users, especially in outdoor contexts, `training with someone` is not only about motivation. It is about comfort and perceived safety.

Source:

- [Female trail runner safety concerns study](https://pmc.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/articles/PMC10815876/)

## 5. Court sports were the clearest initial wedge, but not the whole thesis

The `Courts / Social Sports` teardown remains useful because it showed:

- court sports have strong demand for matchmaking, scheduling and session coordination
- this category already proves users will pay for products that solve `who do I play with and when`

But the stronger insight is broader:

- the same matching job exists outside courts too

Examples:

- finding a ride buddy
- finding a running partner of similar pace
- finding someone for a gym session
- finding a nearby small group for hiking or trail runs

## Why This Fits Strava Specifically

## 1. It fits Strava’s strongest current assets

Strava already has:

- social graph
- activity history
- clubs
- events
- location context
- trust around real activity identity

These assets make partner matching much more native for Strava than for a generic social app.

## 2. It is closer to Strava’s DNA than booking marketplaces

Compared with full booking-led verticals, partner matching is strategically cleaner because it starts from:

- people
- activity
- coordination

instead of:

- venue supply
- payments ops
- local merchant integrations

That makes it a more realistic first wedge.

## 3. It deepens the IRL community thesis

Strava’s broader direction already points toward:

- clubs
- IRL events
- community as motivation

Partner matching is a natural extension of this:

- smaller than clubs
- more practical than feed-only social
- more recurring than one-off events

## Suggested Product Frame

### Recommended name

- `Strava Partner Layer`

### What it does

Helps a user find:

- one partner
- a duo / trio / small group
- a session that fits their sport, level and timing

### Good first use cases

- padel / tennis / pickleball
- running partner by pace
- cycling partner by route type or speed
- beginner gym accountability partner

### Not the right first framing

- “dating for fitness”
- generic chat app
- full booking marketplace from day one

## Best Entry Strategy

The strongest first entry looks like:

- `matchmaking + session coordination`

not:

- full club operations
- full payments stack
- full venue booking infrastructure

### Why

Because this lets Strava leverage what it already has:

- activity graph
- trust
- social identity
- local relevance

without immediately taking on the heaviest marketplace complexity.

## Risks

## 1. It still needs a new UX layer

This cannot be solved by just:

- adding a new activity type
- adding a new club tag
- adding another feed surface

It needs a dedicated flow around:

- who
- when
- where
- level
- confirmation / coordination

## 2. Safety and trust design matter

If the product is about meeting other people in real life, then:

- privacy
- moderation
- trust signals
- matching transparency

become critical.

## 3. It should stay functional, not drift into dating

The value is:

- reducing friction to train
- making exercise easier to start and repeat

not:

- broad social discovery for its own sake

## Strategic Verdict

This direction now looks stronger than a narrow `Courts` thesis.

### Why

- it solves a real and recurring barrier: `no one to train with`
- it fits Strava’s social and IRL direction
- it uses assets Strava already has
- it can start in court sports, but expand into other activities

### Final view

The best version of this idea is:

- not `Strava Courts only`
- but `Strava as a partner-matching and session-coordination layer for active people`

## Short Verdict For Tables

Сильний напрям, бо вирішує реальний barrier `нема з ким тренуватись` і добре лягає в стратегію Strava навколо community та IRL activity. Найкраще виглядає не як тільки courts, а як partner-matching layer для social sports, outdoor і частини gym-сценаріїв.
