# Team + Mentor Presentation Source of Truth

## Purpose

This document is the single source of truth for the **pre-mentor presentation page** that will be built in Lovable.

It should prevent context drift across:

- team discussions
- Lovable generation
- future edits
- new chats with AI tools

This page is **not** the final jury presentation.
It is a working strategic page for:

1. aligning the team
2. building trust with the mentor
3. showing research rigor
4. deciding what to validate next

## Page Goal

The page should communicate:

1. what the assignment is
2. what research we already did
3. which AI tools we used
4. what hypothesis clusters emerged
5. which 3 ideas we want to validate most
6. which paid tools the mentor can help us use
7. what exactly we need to check tomorrow
8. how the team will move from hypothesis sprint to final delivery

## Tone

The tone should feel:

- strategic
- polished
- editorial
- founder / product team style
- visually premium but not flashy for its own sake

It should feel like:

- "we did real work"
- "we know where we are uncertain"
- "we are ready to validate quickly and seriously"

## Language

The page should be written primarily in **Ukrainian**.

Keep these in **English** where it is more natural and useful:

- company names
- product names
- tool names
- hypothesis names when they are short and recognizable
- standard product, strategy, growth, and marketing vocabulary

Examples of terms that may stay in English:

- product-market fit
- roadmap
- growth
- retention
- monetization
- unit economics
- prototype
- evidence quality
- source of truth
- backup
- shortlist

Copy should feel natural for a Ukrainian-speaking product team, not over-translated.

Avoid comic mixed phrasing in the visible copy.

Bad examples:

- `Три bucket-и. Один focus.`
- `Ми рухаємось по roadmap швидко і smart`

Prefer natural Ukrainian wording for structural phrases:

- `три групи гіпотез`
- `один головний фокус`
- `план роботи`
- `що перевіряємо далі`

Use English only where it is truly standard and helpful.

## Visual Direction

The design should be inspired by the previous Cleaner Guru Lovable page:

- clean light background
- soft premium gradients
- strong typography hierarchy
- fixed top navigation
- large hero with one key message
- section-based storytelling
- cards, comparison tables, highlighted takeaways
- subtle motion, not over-animated

### Style Notes

- keep background light, not dark
- use bold headline typography
- use one main gradient accent, but avoid purple-heavy overload
- keep spacing generous
- make sections easy to skim
- emphasize decision-making, not only information density

### 5. Competitor Signals

Show the strongest insight summary from competitors:

Strava should not try to win by "better tracking."
It needs to win through **social graph + identity + community + new use case**.

Also include a **Hypothesis Glossary** directly under this block.

Purpose of the glossary:

- the team and mentor should quickly understand what each idea actually means
- names may stay in English
- explanations should be short, natural, and in Ukrainian
- this block should reduce confusion, not add more abstraction

Recommended format:

- accordion, expandable cards, or compact glossary grid
- each item should include:
  - idea name in English
  - 1 short Ukrainian explanation of what the product would be
  - optionally 1 line on who it is for

Important:

- include all current ideas from the expanded hypothesis universe
- avoid vague phrases
- explain each idea as if the audience sees it for the first time
- keep this block practical and scannable

## Must-Include Content

These points must appear somewhere on the page:

- we used multiple AI tools, not one
- we compared outputs instead of trusting one answer
- we separated strong evidence from weaker signals
- we already have a top 3
- we know what to validate tomorrow
- we have a roadmap to the final defense

## Do Not Do

- do not make it look like the final jury deck
- do not overload with giant paragraphs
- do not present low-confidence ideas as if they are chosen
- do not make the whole page dark or "cyber"
- do not create a generic startup landing page
- do not hide uncertainty; show it clearly and professionally

## Final Preferred Narrative

If the page needs a central narrative, use this:

We explored broadly, synthesized aggressively, and now we are entering a focused validation phase.

The three main bets are:

1. Strava Work
2. Strava Escapes
3. Race Marketplace

The open strategic question is whether the final project should lean toward:

- strongest business case
or
- strongest jury story

Tomorrow's mentor session is designed to answer that question with better evidence.

## Overall Copy Rule

Use **Ukrainian first**, but do not force awkward translation.

Keep English for:

- names
- tools
- frameworks
- established marketing and product terms

The end result should be easy for the team to scan quickly before the mentor session.
It should sound like a strong Ukrainian-speaking product team, not like machine-translated startup slang.
