## What Each Tool Type Is Best For

### 1. Company and Funding Databases

Use these to answer:

- Are startups already attacking this space?
- Is this a funded category or a weak niche?
- Which business models are investors backing?
- Are there recent M&A or category consolidations?
- Is there evidence that the market is hot, crowded, or still forming?

Best tools:

- Crunchbase
- PitchBook
- Dealroom
- CB Insights
- Mattermark
- Owler

### 2. Web Traffic and Web Tech Tools

Use these to answer:

- Which competitors are getting real user attention?
- Where does their traffic come from?
- Do they rely on SEO, direct brand, partnerships, or paid traffic?
- Are they likely consumer-first or B2B-first?
- Which markets or geographies matter most?

Best tools:

- SimilarWeb if acceptable
- Ahrefs
- SpyFu
- Serpstat
- BuiltWith

### 3. Mobile App Market Tools

Use these to answer:

- Which apps in this space actually grow?
- Which monetization model works in mobile?
- Are downloads or revenue concentrated in a few leaders?
- What do app store reviews reveal about unmet user pain?
- Are there app-category signals that support or weaken a hypothesis?

Best tools:

- Sensor Tower
- AppTweak
- data.ai
- App Store / Google Play reviews

## Validation Matrix By Hypothesis

### A. Corporate Wellness / Strava for Work

#### What to validate

- Is corporate wellness still a funded and growing category?
- Which subcategories are strongest: engagement, rewards, preventive health, analytics, social challenges?
- Which vendors dominate: Wellhub, Virgin Pulse, Limeade, BetterMe Business, others?
- Are companies buying generic wellness platforms or more specific engagement products?
- Is there whitespace for a Strava-native social product rather than another HR dashboard?

#### Best tools

- PitchBook
- Crunchbase
- Dealroom
- CB Insights
- Owler
- SimilarWeb or Ahrefs for key vendors
- App Store / mobile tools only if relevant apps have large footprints

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- funded category with active players
- clear demand for engagement and retention, not only benefits administration
- gaps in community mechanics or employee motivation
- examples of successful B2B wellness apps with weak social/community layers

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- heavy market saturation with strong incumbents and little differentiation
- strong enterprise expectation for medical / insurance integration that Strava cannot credibly meet
- traffic and growth concentrated in broad HR platforms instead of focused wellness products

### B. Active Travel / Adventure / Active Experiences

#### What to validate

- Is active travel or outdoor experience planning a growing and monetizable category?
- Which products dominate today: komoot, AllTrails, travel planners, guided-tour platforms?
- Are users looking only for routes, or also for local experiences, itineraries, and bookings?
- Can Strava credibly move from route discovery to destination discovery and trip planning?

#### Best tools

- Crunchbase
- Dealroom
- CB Insights
- Product Hunt
- SimilarWeb or Ahrefs
- Sensor Tower / data.ai / AppTweak
- App Store / Google Play reviews for komoot, AllTrails, similar apps

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- strong app growth or web demand around outdoor planning and active trips
- repeated user complaints about fragmented planning, booking, and discovery
- evidence that route intelligence and community trust matter in travel decisions

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- category dominated by a few specialized leaders with strong lock-in
- little proof users want one integrated product for discovery + booking + community
- evidence that Strava's role is already "good enough" and not monetizable beyond existing routes

### C. Race & Event Marketplace

#### What to validate

- Is local event participation growing?
- Are registration platforms old and weak enough to disrupt?
- Are there fragmented discovery problems that Strava can solve?
- Is the opportunity mostly software for race organizers, or mostly athlete-side discovery?

#### Best tools

- Crunchbase
- PitchBook
- Dealroom
- Owler
- SimilarWeb / Ahrefs for RunSignup, Let's Do This, Eventbrite, race-discovery products
- Sensor Tower and app store reviews if relevant event apps exist

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- clear traffic to event registration/discovery products
- weak UX or fragmented discovery seen in user reviews
- growth in endurance participation and local race ecosystems

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- category structurally unattractive due to operational complexity
- race organizers value incumbents mainly because of back-office operations Strava would struggle to replicate

### D. Coaching / Coach Marketplace

#### What to validate

- Is the coaching market crowded but monetizable?
- Are coaches switching between tools like TrainingPeaks, COROS, Garmin, Final Surge, etc.?
- Is there room for a more social or discovery-driven coach marketplace?

#### Best tools

- Crunchbase
- CB Insights
- Product Hunt
- SimilarWeb / Ahrefs
- App reviews for coaching and training apps

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- users complain that current coaching tools are powerful but clunky
- fragmented discovery of coaches
- social proof and athlete identity emerge as missing value

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- coach-side tools are already entrenched and highly sticky
- athletes choose coaches outside the product workflow entirely

### E. Creator Economy

#### What to validate

- Are there successful fitness creator platforms with meaningful monetization?
- Is the value in subscriptions, paid programs, paid challenges, or community access?
- Would Strava's audience naturally convert to creator-led products?

#### Best tools

- Crunchbase
- Product Hunt
- YC Directory
- SimilarWeb / Ahrefs
- App store review mining for creator-fitness products

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- funded creator fitness products
- strong conversion patterns for creator-led programs
- gaps between social audience and monetization tooling

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- creator businesses depend too much on external media channels, not in-app behavior
- weak evidence that Strava itself is the right point of monetization

### F. Recovery / Longevity

#### What to validate

- Is this space too hardware-driven?
- Can software-only recovery guidance still create enough value?
- Are users already overloaded with WHOOP/Oura/Garmin signals?

#### Best tools

- Crunchbase
- CB Insights
- SimilarWeb
- Sensor Tower / data.ai / AppTweak
- App reviews for WHOOP, Oura companions, recovery apps

#### What evidence would strengthen the idea

- users want better interpretation and habit formation, not just biometrics
- software-led recovery apps still show traction

#### What evidence would weaken the idea

- category too dependent on proprietary sensors and closed ecosystems
- Strava would be only a secondary dashboard, not the core product
