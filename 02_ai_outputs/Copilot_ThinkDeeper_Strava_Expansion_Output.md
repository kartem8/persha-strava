### 04_One-Page Thesis (final recommendation: **Corporate Wellness Platform**)

**Hypothesis**  
**Strava should expand into a B2B Corporate Wellness platform that helps employers run measurable, privacy-first activity programs for employees.**

**Why this market**  
Corporate wellness is a large, growing market as employers invest in employee health to reduce healthcare costs and improve retention. Employers want measurable programs that drive behavior change and can be reported to leadership.

**Why Strava**  
Strava’s habit loops, social graph, challenge mechanics, and activity/GPS data give it a unique right to win: it can deliver engaging employee challenges, credible activity verification, and community-driven motivation at scale.

**Target audience**  
Beachhead: HR/People Ops at mid-size (500–5,000 employees) tech and professional services companies running voluntary wellness programs.

**Product concept**  
A Strava for Workplaces product with: company admin portal, configurable team challenges, aggregated anonymized analytics (participation, minutes active), privacy controls, integrations with HRIS and benefits platforms, and optional employer-branded campaigns.

**Monetization logic**  
Primary: subscription (per-employee or tiered company plans). Secondary: premium analytics, event partnerships, and white-label challenge campaigns.

**Why this is presentation-worthy**  
Clear demo: admin creates a 30-day step/ride challenge; employees join via mobile; live leaderboard and aggregated ROI dashboard show participation and estimated health impact. The demo is visual, social, and emotionally resonant.

**Key risks**  
1. **Privacy & compliance** — employers and employees must trust anonymized reporting and opt-in flows.  
2. **Sales cycle** — B2B sales require longer cycles and proof of ROI.  
3. **Competition** — established corporate wellness vendors and HR platforms.

**Next step**  
Validate employer willingness-to-pay with 8–12 discovery interviews and a landing page MVP offering a pilot program; run a 30-day pilot with one mid-size company to measure engagement and build ROI case.
