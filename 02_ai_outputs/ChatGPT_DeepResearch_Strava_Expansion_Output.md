# 1. Strava Summary

## Current Value Proposition
Strava is a **social fitness platform** that combines activity tracking with social motivation:
- Track activities (GPS-based)
- Share progress with a network
- Compete via segments, challenges, leaderboards

**Core loop:** Track → Share → Compare → Compete → Repeat

---

## Core User Segments

| Segment | Needs | Behavior |
|--------|------|---------|
| Casual fitness users | Motivation, habit-building | Use challenges, social feed |
| Amateur athletes | Performance improvement | Use segments, analytics |
| Hardcore athletes | Structured training | Often supplement with other tools |
| Outdoor explorers | Route discovery | Partial use (weaker vs komoot) |

---

## Core Strengths

**Facts (from product + playbook):**
- Strong social graph
- GPS activity dataset
- Gamification (segments, challenges)
- Clubs and identity layers
- Mobile-first experience
- Freemium subscription model

**Inference:**
- Strongest in **motivation layer**, weaker in **coaching and real-world experiences**
- Under-monetized social graph

---

## Monetization Model

| Type | Details |
|------|--------|
| Freemium | Free core tracking |
| Subscription | ~$10–12/month |
| Paid features | Advanced analytics, routes, segments |

**Inference:**  
No marketplace, B2B, or transaction-based revenue → expansion opportunity

# 5. Top 3 With Tradeoffs

## 1. Coach Marketplace
- **Why it made top 3:** strongest monetization + strategic fit  
- **Main upside:** large market, high willingness to pay  
- **Main downside:** harder to prototype clearly  
- **Biggest risk:** competition from Garmin/COROS  
- **Relative position:** strongest business case, weaker demo vs others  

---

## 2. Local Experiences Marketplace
- **Why it made top 3:** best balance of fit + wow + prototype  
- **Main upside:** leverages social + location (core strengths)  
- **Main downside:** marketplace liquidity challenge  
- **Biggest risk:** safety + supply creation  
- **Relative position:** strongest storytelling and demo potential  

---

## 3. Adventure Trips Marketplace
- **Why it made top 3:** high differentiation + strong narrative  
- **Main upside:** expands into travel category  
- **Main downside:** less core to daily usage  
- **Biggest risk:** operational complexity  
- **Relative position:** higher wow than Coach, weaker fit than Experiences

# 7. One-Page Thesis

## Hypothesis  
Strava should expand into a **local active experiences marketplace** enabling users to discover and join real-world fitness activities.

---

## Why Now
- Growth of experience economy  
- Rising demand for **social fitness**  
- Urban loneliness and need for community  

---

## Why Strava
- Existing **social graph**  
- Rich **location + route data**  
- Strong **identity layer**  

---

## Target Segment
Urban Gen Z & Millennials:
- socially motivated  
- active lifestyle  
- travel frequently  

---

## Product Concept
Marketplace for:
- group runs, rides, hikes  
- local events  
- hosted experiences  

**Core flows:**
1. Discover (map-based feed)  
2. View event (details + route)  
3. Join / pay  
4. Group coordination  

---

## Revenue Model
- Commission per booking (10–20%)  
- Premium host tools  
- Sponsored events  

---

## Wow Factor For Presentation
- Map-based discovery UI  
- Real-world + digital integration  
- Clear story: “Run with locals anywhere”  

---

## Key Risks
- Safety and trust  
- Marketplace liquidity  
- Supply creation  

---

## What To Validate Next
- Will users pay?  
- Will users host?  
- Conversion vs free meetups  
- City-level liquidity
